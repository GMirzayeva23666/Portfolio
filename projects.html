Construction of Logic Gates

Transistors • Breadboards • LEDs

The aim of this project was to gain a practical understanding of how computer hardware operates at a fundamental level by constructing and testing basic logic gates. Through hands-on work with electronic components such as transistors, resistors, LEDs, and breadboards, we explored how logical operations (NOT, AND, and OR) are physically implemented in hardware.

The project also aimed to strengthen our teamwork, problem-solving, and communication skills by collaboratively building circuits, following laboratory safety rules, and explaining working principles of the logic gates through recorded demonstration.

Technology Stack

Power supply • Breadboard • Resistors • Lab tools

Features

Logic gates • Visual output as LEDs • Team-based work

Impact

Understanding of hardware • Practical skills

Hour of AI

AI • Education • Interactive

The aim of this project was to learn more about algorithms and AI and to share this knowledge with a community that had limited access to technology. By organizing and running an Hour of Code event, we introduced participants to coding in a fun and simple way.

The project also helped us improve our teamwork, communication, and teaching skills while supporting a community with fewer technological opportunities.
